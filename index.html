1900s Runabout

Small, lightweight “horseless carriages” were simple.

  • Common power: single- or twin-cylinder engines, ~6–12 hp
  • Controls varied: tiller or primitive steering wheels
  • Open bodies; pneumatic tires; ~20–30 mph top speed

Model T (1908)

Mass production brought car ownership to the middle class.

  • Moving assembly line cut build time from hours to minutes
  • Durable 2.9-L four-cylinder; high ground clearance for rough roads
  • By the mid-1910s the Model T dominated global sales

1920s Luxury Sedan

Closed bodies, electric starters, and smoother multi-cylinder engines became common.

  • Safety glass, heaters, and better lighting
  • Coachbuilt bodies for the wealthy; standardized sedans for the masses
  • Expanding road networks reshaped cities and tourism

1930s Streamliner

Wind-tunnel inspired bodies improved speed, economy, and style.

  • Integrated fenders, sloped grilles, teardrop cues
  • Independent front suspensions, hydraulic brakes on many models
  • Unit-body experiments hinted at modern construction

1940s Post-war

Production resumed after WWII with updated pre-war designs—then rapid innovation.

  • All-steel bodies
  • Automatic transmissions gained popularity
  • Suburban growth fueled car demand

1950s Tailfins

Jet-age styling, V8 power, and comfort features defined the decade.

  • Power steering/brakes, air conditioning on upscale models
  • Distinctive tailfins and chrome; highway cruising at last
  • Safety research begins—seat belts spread late in the 1950s

1960s Compact

Smaller, lighter cars responded to urban needs and new buyers.

  • Unibody construction commonplace; economical four- and six-cylinders
  • Global influences (European/Japanese designs) reach the U.S.
  • Improved rustproofing and reliability expectations

1960s Muscle

Affordable high-horsepower cars captured youth culture and motorsport.

  • Powerful V8s, bold graphics, and performance packages
  • Pony cars > mid-size platforms with big engines
  • Emissions and safety rules at decade’s end reshaped the market
